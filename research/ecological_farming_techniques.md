# Data Harvester Report: Ecological Farming Techniques (Enrichment / Cost-Yield Angle)

Selected ultra-low-cost, high-yield ecological farming techniques from original systems (JADAM, KNF, Fukuoka) that maximize soil health, microbial diversity, and crop productivity without commercial inputs.

## 1. JADAM Microbial Solution (JMS)
* **Core Principle**: Mass-cultivates localized indigenous microorganisms using simple, extremely low-cost food sources. Replaces expensive commercial inoculants.
* **Cost-Yield Impact**: Costs under $1 per 500 liters. Promotes robust root development, disease resistance, and rapid soil organic matter decomposition, leading to higher yields.
* **Recipe / Steps**:
  1. Fill a 500-liter container with non-chlorinated water.
  2. Place 0.5 kg of leaf mold (collected from a local healthy forest floor containing diverse indigenous microbes) into a fine mesh bag.
  3. Add 1 kg of boiled potatoes (or boiled sweet potatoes/highly cooked rice) as a food source into the same or a separate bag.
  4. Suspend the bags in the water. Thoroughly knead the potato bag to release starches into the water.
  5. Add 0.5 kg of sea salt (provides trace minerals) and dissolve.
  6. Keep the water temperature around 20–25°C. Do not use an aerator; let it ferment naturally.
  7. Foam will form and peak (usually in 24–72 hours depending on temperature). Apply at the peak stage.
  8. **Application**: Dilute 1:10 or 1:20 with water and apply to soil.

## 2. KNF Indigenous Microorganisms 4 (IMO-4)
* **Core Principle**: Transitions forest-floor microbes (IMO-1) through solid-state fermentation phases (IMO-2 with brown sugar, IMO-3 with wheat bran/rice bran) to a robust soil-ready compost carrier (IMO-4).
* **Cost-Yield Impact**: Uses cheap agricultural byproducts (rice bran, local soil) to inoculate massive plots with highly-adapted local beneficial fungi and bacteria. Greatly improves soil structure and nutrient availability.
* **Recipe / Steps**:
  1. **IMO-1 to IMO-3**: Mix KNF IMO-2 (IMO-1 stabilized 1:1 with brown sugar) with water and nutrients (FPJ, BRV, OHN), then blend thoroughly into a bulk pile of rice bran or wheat bran (65% moisture). Let it ferment, turning to keep temperature under 50°C, until covered in white mycelium (IMO-3).
  2. **IMO-4 Preparation**: Combine 1 part IMO-3 with 1 part local field soil and 1 part forest soil (clay soil).
  3. Adjust moisture to 65% by spraying with KNF liquid inputs (FPJ, BRV, OHN) diluted in water.
  4. Create a pile 30–40 cm deep, cover with straw or mats to keep dark and retain moisture.
  5. Turn the pile every 1–2 days to control temperature. Let it ferment for 5–7 days until it stabilizes and smells earthy.
  6. **Application**: Broadcast 200–500 kg per hectare onto field soil before planting, then cover with mulch.

## 3. JADAM Liquid Fertilizer (JLF)
* **Core Principle**: Anaerobic fermentation of local wild grass, crop residues, or organic waste using leaf mold and sea salt to create customized nitrogen, potassium, or nutrient-specific fertilizers.
* **Cost-Yield Impact**: Zero-cost, self-sufficient fertilizer production. Completely replaces synthetic NPK while recycling local farm waste.
* **Recipe / Steps**:
  1. Fill a plastic barrel (e.g., 200 liters) half-full with raw organic matter (e.g., wild grass for nitrogen, wild fruits for potassium, fish waste for amino acids).
  2. Add a handful of forest leaf mold (to supply decomposing microbes) and 0.5% to 1% sea salt relative to water volume.
  3. Fill the barrel with water, leaving a small space at the top.
  4. Seal the barrel tightly with a lid to maintain anaerobic conditions.
  5. Let it ferment. For grass, it is ready in 1–3 months (summer) or longer. The longer it ferments, the more refined the nutrients become.
  6. **Application**: Dilute 1:100 to 1:500 with water and apply to soil or as a foliar spray.

## 4. Fukuoka Seed Clay Balls (Nendo Dango)
* **Core Principle**: Encase diverse seeds in clay and compost pellets, allowing them to germinate naturally only when environmental conditions (rainfall, temperature) are perfect.
* **Cost-Yield Impact**: Eliminates tilling, weeding, transplanting, and chemical soil preparation costs. Drastically reduces labor and mechanical costs while maintaining high polyculture yields.
* **Recipe / Steps**:
  1. Mix 5 parts red clay (sifted, dry) with 1 part organic compost/soil.
  2. Add a diverse mixture of seeds (e.g., grains, clover cover crops, vegetables).
  3. Add water slowly while kneading the mixture into a dough-like consistency.
  4. Roll the mixture into small balls (about 1–2 cm in diameter).
  5. Dry the clay balls thoroughly in the shade for several days until completely hard.
  6. **Application**: Scatter the clay balls directly onto unplowed land before the rainy season.
